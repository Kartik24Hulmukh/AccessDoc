# Practitioner evaluation protocol

## Goal
Determine whether converting scanner output into a narrative PDF saves review time without hiding uncertainty.

## Ten-session protocol
1. Recruit five accessibility practitioners and five agency report owners.
2. Give each participant the same synthetic axe JSON and a reference report brief.
3. Ask them to generate, inspect, and correct a report without assistance.
4. Record completion, correction count, elapsed time, confidence, and whether they would use it again.
5. Ask which statement in the PDF could be misread as a legal or conformance claim.

## Pass gates
- At least 8 of 10 complete the flow without intervention.
- Median report correction count is at most two.
- No participant interprets the output as certification.
- At least 4 of 10 ask to test the tool on a second real but non-sensitive export.

These gates evaluate usefulness, not legal compliance or accessibility conformance. The PDF remains an editorial draft requiring qualified review.
